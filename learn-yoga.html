Chapter 1

Roots of Wisdom

Understanding Yoga's Essence and Language

What is Yoga?

Yoga is an ancient physical, mental, and spiritual practice that originated in India. The word 'yoga' derives from Sanskrit and means to join or to unite, symbolizing the union of body and consciousness.

Core Philosophy

Yoga is more than just exercise; it's a holistic system for living. While the West often focuses on the physical poses (Asanas), traditional yoga includes ethical precepts, breath control, and meditation.

Sanskrit: The Language of Yoga

Most yoga terms are in Sanskrit, an ancient Indo-European language. Learning these terms helps deepen your understanding of the practice. Click the cards to learn key terms:

Asana

Physical Pose

Originally meaning "seat", it now refers to any of the physical postures practiced in yoga.

Pranayama

Breath Control

The practice of controlling the breath, which is considered the source of our life force (Prana).

Drishti

Focused Gaze

A specific point of focus for the eyes used during yoga poses to improve concentration and balance.

Namaste

I bow to you

A respectful greeting. "The divine light in me bows to the divine light in you."

Pranayama: Harnessing Your Life Force

Pranayama Principles

In yoga, the breath is the bridge between the body and the mind. By consciously regulating our breath, we can influence our nervous system, calming the mind or energizing the body.

The 8 Limbs of Yoga

Physical poses are just one of the eight "limbs" described by Patanjali in the Yoga Sutras:

  1. Yama: Ethical standards (how we treat others)
  2. Niyama: Self-discipline (how we treat ourselves)
  3. Asana: Physical postures
  4. Pranayama: Breath control
  5. Pratyahara: Sensory withdrawal
  6. Dharana: Concentration
  7. Dhyana: Meditation
  8. Samadhi: State of bliss/union
Chapter 4

Flows & Traditions

Exploring Different Styles of Yoga

Yoga Traditions

While all yoga shares common roots, different styles have evolved to emphasize different aspects of the practice.

🔥 Ashtanga

Focus: Rigorous, structured series of poses

Style: Always follows the same sequence. High intensity and focuses on internal heat.

🌊 Vinyasa

Focus: Flowing movement synchronized with breath

Style: Creative sequences. Poses transition smoothly from one to another (often called "Flow").

🧘 Hatha

Focus: Foundation and alignment

Style: Slower pace, holding poses for longer. Great for beginners.

❄️ Yin

Focus: Deep connective tissues

Style: Passive poses held for 3-5 minutes. Focuses on flexibility and mindfulness.
